### **MEMORANDUM**

**To:** All Teams
**From:** Leadership
**Date:** 2025-11-11
**Subject:** Communication Playbook: High-Impact Phrases for Core Domains

This document outlines key phrases that drive clarity and efficiency in our communications. Integrating them will help us align faster and convey complex ideas concisely. Please adopt these expressions in discussions related to Technology, Business, and Investment.

---

### **1. Technology**
*Focus on system design, architecture, and execution.*

1.  **"At its core, it's a trade-off between X and Y."**
    *   **Use when:** Framing a technical decision. It shows you have weighed competing factors (e.g., performance vs. development speed) and are making a deliberate choice, not just following a trend.

2.  **"This allows us to decouple A from B."**
    *   **Use when:** Justifying an architectural change. This explains the need to improve system maintainability and scalability by reducing dependencies.

3.  **"The bottleneck is not X, but rather Y."**
    *   **Use when:** Analyzing a performance issue or delay. It demonstrates a precise, data-informed approach to problem-solving by identifying the root cause.

4.  **"It's not production-ready yet."**
    *   **Use when:** Managing expectations about a new feature or system. This clarifies that while a function may work, it still needs to meet our standards for reliability, security, and scalability.

5.  **"We need to dogfood our own product."**
    *   **Use when:** Advocating for internal testing. This drives a user-centric culture by ensuring we experience our product firsthand to identify issues and validate its value before release.

---

### **2. Business**
*Focus on value, strategy, and execution.*

1.  **"What's our value proposition here?"**
    *   **Use when:** Starting a project or designing a product. This question centers the discussion on customer needs and the unique value we provide.

2.  **"Let's focus on the low-hanging fruit first."**
    *   **Use when:** Setting priorities or creating a roadmap. This strategy targets quick, high-ROI wins to build momentum and validate our direction.

3.  **"This is not scalable."**
    *   **Use when:** Evaluating a business model or process. It raises a critical concern about whether growth will require a proportional increase in costs, which could limit long-term potential.

4.  **"We need to align on the key metrics."**
    *   **Use when:** Kicking off a cross-functional project. This ensures all teams are working toward the same measurable goals, preventing misalignment and wasted effort.

5.  **"What's the go-to-market strategy?"**
    *   **Use when:** Planning a product launch. This moves the conversation beyond product features to the critical elements of pricing, distribution, marketing, and sales required for commercial success.

---

### **3. Investment**
*Focus on risk, return, and market dynamics.*

1.  **"What's the TAM (Total Addressable Market)?"**
    *   **Use when:** Assessing a new opportunity. This question focuses the analysis on the market's ceiling, a key factor determining the potential for long-term growth.

2.  **"They have a strong moat."**
    *   **Use when:** Analyzing a company's competitive advantage. This refers to its defensible strengths (e.g., brand, network effects, patents) that protect its long-term profitability.

3.  **"It's currently priced in."**
    *   **Use when:** Discussing the impact of public news on an asset. This indicates an understanding that the current market price already reflects all known information, and that new opportunities arise from unappreciated factors.

4.  **"What's your investment thesis?"**
    *   **Use when:** Pitching or evaluating an investment. This demands a clear, logical, and testable framework for the investment decision, moving beyond speculation to a structured argument.

5.  **"This is a high-risk, high-return play."**
    *   **Use when:** Categorizing an investment opportunity. This clearly defines the asset's risk profile, which is fundamental for portfolio construction and risk management.
