Time Perspectives at Work**

**Characters:**
*   **Lily:** A young professional, often puzzled by colleagues' different work styles. (一个年轻嘅职场人士，成日唔明点解同事工作方式咁唔同)
*   **Ken:** A more experienced colleague, with an interest in psychology. (一个有经验嘅同事，对心理学有兴趣)

**(Scene: Lily and Ken are having a casual chat during their lunch break in the office pantry.)**
**(场景：Lily 同 Ken 喺公司茶水间食晏，轻松咁倾偈。)**

**Lily:** Ken, I'm so confused sometimes. Like, our colleague John, he's always <code><mark>living for the moment</mark></code>, super spontaneous. But then there's Sarah, who plans everything six months in advance! How do people manage their time so differently?

*   **粤语讲解：** Lily 讲到佢对同事唔同工作方式嘅困惑。`living for the moment` (活在当下) 呢个词，就系指只顾眼前嘅快乐，唔多考虑将来。佢想知点解大家对时间嘅睇法咁唔同。

**Ken:** Ah, that's a classic example of **Zimbardo's Time Perspective Psychology**. It's not really about *managing* time, but how we **psychologically perceive, experience, and internalise** time. It's like a mental filter for how we look at the past, present, and future.
**(Ken：吓，呢个就系**津巴多时间心理学**嘅经典例子啦。其实唔系真系讲紧点样“管理”时间，而系我哋心理上点样**感知、体验同内化**时间（过去、现在、未来）。佢就好似一个心理滤镜咁，影响我哋点样睇过去、现在同将来。)**

*   **粤语讲解：** Ken 介绍咗**津巴多时间心理学** (Zimbardo's Time Perspective Psychology) 呢个核心概念。佢强调呢个理论系讲紧人对时间嘅**心理感知** (psychologically perceive)，而唔系实际嘅时间管理技巧。`internalise` (内化) 呢个词，就系指将外部嘅嘢变成自己内心嘅一部分。

**Lily:** A mental filter? So, it's about our attitude towards time, not just our to-do lists?
**(Lily：心理滤镜？咁即系话，系我哋对时间嘅态度，而唔系净系我哋嘅待办事项清单？)**

*   **粤语讲解：** Lily 尝试理解 Ken 嘅解释，佢捉到重点，就系“态度” (attitude) 而唔系“清单” (to-do lists)。

**Ken:** Exactly! This **psychological time perspective** shapes our **attitude, beliefs, and sense of control** over different time dimensions. The key insight is that our **psychological control over time** directly drives our behaviour.
**(Ken：冇错！呢种**心理时间视角** (psychological time perspective) 塑造咗我哋对时间嘅**态度、信念同掌控感**。最重要嘅一点就系，我哋对时间嘅**心理掌控情况** (psychological control over time) 直接影响我哋做嘢嘅方式。)**

*   **粤语讲解：** Ken 进一步解释，人对时间嘅**心理掌控情况** (psychological control over time) 先系决定行为模式嘅关键。`attitude` (态度)、`beliefs` (信念) 同 `sense of control` (掌控感) 都系呢个理论嘅重要元素。

**Lily:** So, John might have a "Present Hedonistic" view, always chasing fun? And Sarah is "Future-Oriented"?
**(Lily：咁即系话，阿John可能系“现在享乐”型，成日追住玩？而阿Sarah就系“未来取向”型？)**

*   **粤语讲解：** Lily 尝试将同事套入津巴多理论嘅主要时间视角类型。
    *   `Present Hedonistic` (现在享乐)：指只顾当下快乐，回避痛苦。
    *   `Future-Oriented` (未来取向)：指关注未来目标同回报，有规划、自律。

**Ken:** You <code><mark>hit the nail on the head</mark></code>! John probably focuses on immediate pleasure, which can lead to being impulsive or even <code><mark>putting things off until tomorrow</mark></code>. Sarah, on the other hand, is all about future goals and rewards, so she's naturally more disciplined and good at **delayed gratification**.
**(Ken：你<code><mark>讲中晒</mark></code>！阿John可能就系专注眼前嘅快乐，所以佢会比较冲动，甚至会<code><mark>将啲嘢拖到听日先做</mark></code>。阿Sarah就唔同啦，佢系为咗将来嘅目标同回报而努力，所以佢自然会自律啲，亦都擅长**延迟满足** (delayed gratification)。)**

*   **粤语讲解：** Ken 肯定咗 Lily 嘅判断。`hit the nail on the head` (讲中晒) 系一个常用嘅惯用语，指一语中的，说中要害。`putting things off until tomorrow` (将啲嘢拖到听日先做) 就系指拖延。`delayed gratification` (延迟满足) 系指为咗长远目标而放弃眼前嘅享受。

**Lily:** What about people who always <code><mark>dwell on the past</mark></code>? I know someone who can't seem to move on from old mistakes.
**(Lily：咁有啲人成日<code><mark>沉溺喺过去</mark></code>又点算呢？我识得有人好似永远都放唔低以前嘅错事咁。)**

*   **粤语讲解：** Lily 问到“过去消极”型嘅人。`dwell on the past` (沉溺喺过去) 系指老是想着过去嘅事，尤其系唔开心嘅。

**Ken:** That could be a "Past Negative" perspective. They focus on unpleasant past experiences, which often leads to avoidance and anxiety. Then there's "Past Positive," who cherish good memories and traditions. And "Present Fatalistic," who feel powerless about the future, just <code><mark>taking things as they come</mark></code> without much effort.
**(Ken：嗰个可能就系“过去消极”型啦。佢哋成日谂住以前唔开心嘅经历，搞到成日想逃避同焦虑。另外仲有“过去积极”型，佢哋就系珍惜美好嘅回忆同传统。仲有“现在宿命”型，佢哋觉得将来系命运安排，自己做唔到啲乜，所以就<code><mark>随遇而安</mark></code>，唔会点努力。)**

*   **粤语讲解：** Ken 解释咗“过去消极” (Past Negative) 同“过去积极” (Past Positive) 两种视角，仲有“现在宿命” (Present Fatalistic) 型。`taking things as they come` (随遇而安) 系指顺其自然，唔多计划。

**Lily:** Wow, this really <code><mark>sheds some light on</mark></code> why people behave so differently. So, is there an ideal time perspective?
**(Lily：哗，呢个真系<code><mark>帮我搞清楚</mark></code>点解啲人行为咁唔同啦。咁有冇一个最理想嘅时间视角㗎？)**

*   **粤语讲解：** Lily 觉得呢个理论好有启发性。`sheds some light on` (帮我搞清楚) 系指帮助理解，使事情变得更清晰。

**Ken:** Zimbardo suggests a **balanced time perspective** is ideal. It's not about being one type, but having a good mix: high past positive, high future, moderate present hedonistic, and low past negative and present fatalistic. It helps you <code><mark>strike a balance</mark></code> between enjoying the present, learning from the past, and planning for the future.
**(Ken：津巴多就建议**平衡嘅时间视角** (balanced time perspective) 系最理想嘅。唔系净系做一种人，而系要好好咁混合：多啲过去积极、多啲未来取向、适度嘅现在享乐，同埋少啲过去消极同现在宿命。咁样可以帮你<code><mark>取得平衡</mark></code>，既可以享受当下，又可以从过去学习，仲可以为将来做好计划。)**

*   **粤语讲解：** Ken 提到“平衡嘅时间视角” (balanced time perspective) 系理想状态，并解释咗具体嘅组合。`strike a balance` (取得平衡) 系指在不同事物之间找到一个合适的点。

**Lily:** That makes perfect sense. It's not about being a robot, but about understanding your own mental filters. Thanks, Ken, you've given me a lot of <code><mark>food for thought</mark></code>!
**(Lily：呢个完全讲得通。唔系要我哋做机器人，而系要理解自己嘅心理滤镜。多谢你呀，Ken，你真系畀咗我好多<code><mark>值得思考嘅嘢</mark></code>！)**

*   **粤语讲解：** Lily 再次感谢 Ken，并表示会好好思考。`food for thought` (值得思考嘅嘢) 系指有启发性嘅观点。
