以下是围绕 **"Consumerism in developed vs. developing countries: Drivers and societal consequences"**（发达国家与发展中国家的消费主义：驱动因素与社会后果）这一话题的**写作范文。

中英双语对照，旨在帮助 DSE 考生构建论证框架。

---

### **Part 1: Drivers of Consumerism (消费主义的驱动因素)**

此部分探讨“为什么”这两个地区的消费主义会兴起。

#### **1. Economic Factors (经济因素)**

- **Developed Countries (发达国家):**
  - **High Disposable Income:** Citizens possess greater purchasing power, fueling non-essential expenditure on luxury goods and experiences.
    - (高可支配收入：公民拥有更强的购买力，推动了对奢侈品和体验的非必要支出。)
  - **Saturation of Markets:** Basic needs are met, leading corporations to create "artificial desires" through planned obsolescence (products designed to break or become outdated quickly).
    - (市场饱和：基本需求得到满足，导致企业通过“计划性报废”制造“虚假需求”。)
- **Developing Countries (发展中国家):**
  - **Rapid Rise of the Middle Class:** Economic growth has created a new demographic with increasing purchasing power, eager to emulate Western lifestyles.
    - (中产阶级迅速崛起：经济增长创造了具备新购买力的人群，渴望模仿西方生活方式。)
  - **Urbanization:** Migration to cities exposes individuals to modern retail environments and consumption-oriented urban cultures.
    - (城市化：向城市迁移使个人接触到现代零售环境和以消费为导向的城市文化。)

#### **2. Psychological and Cultural Factors (心理与文化因素)**

- **Developed Countries:**
  - **Status Anxiety:** Consumption is often driven by the need to signal social status and <mark class="hltr-purple">maintain a certain image among peers</mark>.
    - (地位焦虑：消费往往源于在社会同龄人中炫耀社会地位和维持某种形象的需求。)
- **Developing Countries:**
  - **Globalization and Westernization:** Exposure to global media <mark class="hltr-purple">portrays</mark> material wealth as a symbol of success and modernity.
    - (全球化与西化：接触全球媒体将物质财富描绘为成功和现代化的象征。)
  - **<mark class="hltr-purple">The "Bandwagon Effect"</mark>:** A desire to integrate into the global community drives the adoption of global brands and trends.
    - (“从众效应”：融入国际社区的愿望推动了对全球品牌和潮流的采纳。)

---

### **Part 2: Societal Consequences (社会后果)**

此部分探讨消费主义带来了“什么”影响。

#### **1. Environmental Impact (环境影响)**

- **Developed Countries:** High per capita consumption generates massive amounts of waste and carbon emissions. These nations often export their waste (e-waste, plastic) to developing countries.
  - (高人均消费产生大量废物和碳排放。这些国家经常将废物（电子垃圾、塑料）出口到发展中国家。)
- **Developing Countries:** Often <mark class="hltr-purple">bear the brunt of environmental degradation</mark> due to lax regulations on waste disposal and the manufacturing of cheap goods for global markets.
  - (往往首当其冲承受环境恶化的后果，原因在于废物处理法规宽松以及为全球市场制造廉价商品。)

#### **2. Social Stratification and Inequality (社会分层与不平等)**

- **Both Contexts:** Consumerism can exacerbate the wealth gap. The poor may fall into debt traps trying to maintain a consumerist lifestyle, while the elite continue to accumulate wealth.
  - (消费主义可能加剧贫富差距。穷人可能为了维持消费主义生活方式而陷入债务陷阱，而精英阶层则继续积累财富。)
- **Developing Countries:** <mark class="hltr-purple">Conspicuous consumption</mark> may widen the divide between the urban elite and the rural poor, <mark class="hltr-purple">leading to social friction</mark>.
  - (炫耀性消费可能会拉大城市精英与农村贫困人口之间的差距，导致社会摩擦。)

#### **3. Psychological and Individual Well-being (心理与个人福祉)**

- **The Materialism Paradox:** Studies suggest that a focus on material acquisition is linked to lower life satisfaction and higher rates of depression/anxiety in both developed and developing nations.
  - (唯物主义悖论：研究表明，专注于物质索取与生活满意度降低以及发达国家和发展中国家较高的抑郁/焦虑率有关。)
- **Loss of Traditional Values:** In developing nations, the influx of consumer goods may erode traditional cultural values and community bonds.
  - (传统价值观流失：在发展中国家，消费品的涌入可能会侵蚀传统文化价值观和社区纽带。)

---

### **Synthesis/Critical Thinking (适合 Conclusion 的升华)**

- **The Cycle of Exploitation:** Consumerism creates a cycle where developed nations consume while developing nations produce and suffer the environmental costs, highlighting a need for **ethical consumption** and **global equity**.
  - (剥削循环：消费主义创造了一个“发达国家消费、发展中国家生产并承担环境成本”的循环，凸显了对**道德消费**和**全球公平**的需求。)
- **Redefining Progress:** Both societies must question whether GDP and consumption levels are the true metrics of development, or if **sustainability** and **well-being** should be prioritized.
  - (重新定义进步：两个社会都必须质疑 GDP 和消费水平是否是衡量发展的真正标准，还是应该优先考虑**可持续性**和**福祉**。)
