"Drawing on the Right Side of the Brain: The Original Drawing Book for Anyone, Anytime, Anywhere" is a popular drawing guide written by Betty Edwards, originally published in 1979. Here's a summary and some key points from the book:

**Summary:**
The book introduces readers to the concept that everyone can draw, not just artists, when they approach drawing using their right brain – the creative hemisphere responsible for visual and spatial processing. Edwards provides practical techniques for unlocking one's artistic potential through simple, step-by-step exercises.

**Key Points:**

1. **Approach drawing as a process**: Edwards encourages readers to focus on the enjoyment of drawing rather than worrying about the outcome or trying to create perfect replicas of subjects.
2. **Embrace mistakes**: She teaches students to view errors as opportunities for learning and growth instead of something to be feared or corrected.
3. **Use simple shapes**: The book demonstrates how complex images can be broken down into basic shapes like circles, squares, and rectangles.
4. **Observe and practice**: Edwards emphasizes the importance of closely observing subjects and practicing different drawing exercises to develop artistic skills.
5. **Right-brain thinking**: She encourages readers to draw using their right brain by focusing on visual cues and emotions rather than logical analysis.

**Drawing Techniques:**

* **Line method**: Tracing outlines or contours of objects with a single line.
* **Negative drawing**: Creating images by outlining the spaces around subjects, leaving the shape you want to draw blank.
* **Shadow method**: Using shading to create depth and dimension without detailed lines.
在这句话中，“shading”指的是使用明暗变化来表现物体的立体感和深度，而不是依靠详细的线条。简单来说，就是通过不同深浅的阴影来创造图像的立体效果和层次感。

 Roger Shank, a cognitive scientist, stated that "People are born story understanding rather than logic." Here's the context and interpretation of this quote:

**Context:**
Roger Shank made this remark while discussing how humans process information differently depending on their age and developmental stage. He argues that storytelling plays a crucial role in learning throughout our lives.

> [!cite]
> 在这句话中，“developmental”一词指的是与成长或发展相关的。具体来说，它指的是人在不同成长阶段（如儿童期、青少年期、成年期等）的认知和心理发展的特点。因此，“developmental stage”（发展阶段）指的是人在成长过程中经历的不同阶段，在这些不同的阶段，人的信息处理方式可能会有所不同。

**Interpretation:**
1. **Storytelling vs Logic**: Shank suggests that we're naturally inclined to understand stories better than abstract logic or facts because narratives engage our emotions, imagination, and social connections.
2. **Developmental perspective**: From infancy onward, humans learn through storytelling. This preference for narrative understanding evolves along with our cognitive abilities but remains a fundamental aspect of learning throughout life.
3. **Cultural importance**: Shank's statement highlights the enduring significance of storytelling across cultures as a means of conveying knowledge, values, and traditions from generation to generation.

In essence, Roger Shank's quote emphasizes that humans naturally understand stories rather than logic because narratives engage our emotions, imagination, and social connections more effectively than abstract facts alone. This understanding helps explain why storytelling remains an essential tool for learning throughout our lives and across different cultures.

>

> 2. **Record emotional cues**:
> 	* Jot down short story-like notes about how certain situations made you feel.
> 	* Write about moments when your feelings influenced your decisions, attitudes, or actions.
> 3. **Connect personal experiences**: Tie new ideas from books or resources to your own experiences and emotions.
> 	* Example: Instead of just noting a principle like "Practice > Perfection," write about how aiming for perfection sometimes hindered your progress during a project.
> 
> Here's an example of incorporating storytelling into work journaling:
> 
> **Before (Fact-based Work Journal Entry):**
> 
> * Completed task A in X minutes.
> * Learned new method Y, which improved my efficiency by Z%.
> 
> **After (Storytelling Work Journal Entry):**
> 
> * *Struggled initially with task A. Frustration grew as I tried to force perfection. Eventually realized that progress mattered more than precision. Finally completed it in X minutes.*
> * *Method Y took some time to understand, but once the light bulb moment happened, it revolutionized my workflow. The satisfaction of finding the right approach made the learning curve worthwhile.*
> 
> **Actionable Steps:**
> 
> 1. **Daily reflection**: Spend 5-10 minutes each day recalling emotional moments from your workday. Jot down brief stories or anecdotes illustrating these moments.
> 2. **Weekly review**: Set aside around 30 minutes weekly to read through your storytelling work journal entries, reflecting on emotions and experiences.
> 	* Identify patterns, insights, or progress made since the last review.
> 	* Reconnect with feelings and decisions from past weeks.
> 3. **Monthly or Quarterly Reflection**:
> 	* Review longer intervals in your storytelling work journal for broader trends, personal growth, or emotional breakthroughs.
> 	* Connect your insights back to your own experiences and values.
> 
> By incorporating these steps into your work journaling routine, you'll enhance your ability to appreciate emotions, understand experiences deeply, and cultivate storytelling abilities that serve both yourself and others.
